Order Of Loss Instructions

Land / Sea / Amphibious radio buttons: Select type of battle. "Amphibious" does not include cruisers or battleships used for naval bombardment, as they cannot be removed as casualties.

"X" - removes line.

"+" - creates new copy of line.

"Transport" - (or other unit) - unit name

"-1" - number of units in OOL. "-1" is a special instruction meaning to take all remaining units of the controlling power / unit type.

"#(number)🠞" - assigned number, in order, of priority of preservation. "Transport" is listed as #1, so is preserved first, and taken as a casualty last.

"1" - after all units in the current index element are remomved, this number references the next index that will have units removed. (Note: Will fix this later to be less confusing); "2" would make more sense to readers, or perhaps change above to #(0)🠞".

#⭮: (number) - number of loops to execute. "-1" is a special instruction meaning to maximize number of loops.

OOL Priority: (number) - Order Of Loss Priority.

In the pictured example, transports are lost last; they have the highest priority of preservation and the lowest OOL priority.

Priority lines 3 and 4 list, respectively, 1 carrier, 2 fighters. (note: double check logic for accuracy). After two fighters are removed, a carrier is removed; then the instruction loops so fighters are removed then a carrier, etc.

Priority lines 5 and 6 list, respectively, 1 carrier, 1 fighter. After groups of 2 fighters and 1 carrier are maximized then removed from actual unit count, groups of 1 fighter and 1 carrier are defined. Priority lines 7 and 8 serve a similar purpose; to handle fighters and carriers that remain unallocated after the groups of (2 fighter 1 carrier) are removed.
